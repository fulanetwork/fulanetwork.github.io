Grants

Fula Governance Association awards grants to independent teams whose work adds value to the Fula network.

How grants work

The Fula network is built and maintained by independent contributors rather than by a single company. Fula Governance Association funds that work through grants. A grantee remains a separate, independent entity: it receives a grant to deliver work that benefits the network, and it does not gain governance rights, decision-making authority, or any authority to bind the association.

Who can apply

Individuals, teams and companies building something that adds value to the Fula network — software, infrastructure, tooling, research, documentation, community or ecosystem work.

What we look for

A clear contribution to the network and its users, a team able to deliver it, and a scope that can be assessed against defined milestones.

What a grant is not

A grant is not employment, not a service contract, and not a stake in the association. Grantees act independently and on their own account.

How to start

For more information on available grants, contact Fula Governance members if you have something that adds value to the network.

Congratulations to Functionland Inc on receiving a long-term grant from the Fula Governance community. Functionland Inc is a separate and independent entity, acts independently, and is not responsible for token related matters. Its own products are available at fx.land.
